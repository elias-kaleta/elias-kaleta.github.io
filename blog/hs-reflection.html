Relfecting on Human Skills

The thing that surprised me the most about the human skills learning modules was realising how much exposure I have had to a lot of the content in the past I think growing up I often found myself only being able to speak to myself about things I was feeling or experiencing so I would often find myself researching into psychology and the reasoning behind why I was feeling a certain way which often led me to quite a few of the concepts that we have gone through on these human skills modules.

It is for this reason that I feel pretty confident in my current knowledge on emotional intelligence and a growth mindset, but I am definitely aware of my lack of skill in listening and looping which part I feel relates to my ADHD.

These all tie into my own personal values of not wanting to hinge on other people's issues with my own and try to decipher things on my own instead of reaching out to others which I feel is very visible in my learning process and workflows during these past sprints. I feel going over these concepts again has helped me in realising my pitfalls and I do feel like I have been getting better and reaching out for help when needed but definitely need to work on it more during the boot camp.

What are some new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

I've come to realise that I try to be self sufficient to a fault. I previously wrote about how I try to follow stocism but its become clear that this leads to rather self-destructive habits which hinder my efficiency and at times, my motivation. This also emphasised how my ego encourages me to help others but not accept help from them. I overthink it to the point I fear causing an inconvenience to them or embarrasing myself by having such a *simple* issue.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

They all come down to accountability and social responsibility in the coding process. Programming is a very social-reliant practice as it involves a variety of parties all working towards one overall goal. Values, empathy and self-awareness all create an environment optimised for development, troubleshooting and motivation.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Knowing of a concept is not enough, it is the reemergence of the concepts in practice that cause positive lasting change. I have familiarised myself with many mindsets and psychology terms but reading about it and living it are very different things, knowledge can you to understand but experience is what teaches you how to react.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Taking the time to analyse myself and write it down, it is often done in my head for no one else to take notice of. When writing it down, it seems much more real and forces me to fully articulate my thoughts as opposed to a thought where I just imply many things without truly processing it.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Coding in general needs to be social to some extent, it can be frustrating which can lead to a lot of self doubt but personal and social understanding encourages us to learn healthier habits.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I definitely do not think it is a waste of time. They allowed helthy habits to be fresh in my mind which made it easier to find solutions to my problems like forcing myself to ask for help if I needed it. It also gave a break in between coding which I feel would have become too overwhelming without the breathers in between. In saying that, at some points I would find myself becoming more fustrated with the Human Skills tasks the personal aspect and articulation of my thoughts and feelings can sometimes require more mental effort than coding (coding can be troubleshooted but thoughts and feelings are rather unique, involving high levels of introspection).
