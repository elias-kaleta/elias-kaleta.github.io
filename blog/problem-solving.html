Problem Solving

My experience with a 'simple' problem:

I was working on this JavaScript function that was supposed to make the first letter of each word in a sentence uppercase. Seemed pretty straightforward, right? Well, not exactly.

I was told to use this thing called a .map function to do the letter conversions. But here's the thing – I just couldn't figure out how to use it. It felt like I was going in circles, thinking a simple for loop or forEach loop would have been easier. That frustration crept in as I hit a wall. I tried looking at the documentation, but it just made me more confused. The more I tried to apply the map function, the more annoyed I got.

Eventually, I gave up and turned to YouTube for help. After watching a short 3-minute video, everything suddenly made sense. The syntax of the map function clicked, and I finished the task with a sense of relief. It felt good to finally get it, but I also felt a bit silly for struggling for so long.

This whole experience taught me something important. While written guides are helpful, sometimes a video tutorial can be a game-changer, especially for tricky concepts. It's all about finding what works best for you and making coding a little less of a headache.

My experience with an 'elegant' problem.

I encountered a challenge that required adding each student from an array to a gradebook object. The catch? The student's name had to be the key, with an empty object as the value. As I delved into the task, I stumbled upon the dilemma of referencing a key in an object within a for loop. I got confused on whether to use dot notation or square brackets. Turning to the internet for answers proved to be a maze of its own, as I didn’t know what I didn’ know!

Frustrated but determined, I decided to seek assistance from a friend. And oh boy, what a difference it made! With a bit of human interaction and a fresh perspective, my confusion melted away in no time. It hit me that sometimes, the quickest path to clarity is through a simple conversation with someone who can provide personalized guidance.

This experience left me with a powerful lesson in the art of problem-solving. Elegant solutions often emerge not from endless online quests but from the collaborative spirit of seeking help from others. So, don't shy away from reaching out for a helping hand when you hit a coding roadblock.
